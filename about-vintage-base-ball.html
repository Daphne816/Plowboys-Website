Vintage Base Ball FAQs

What is Vintage Base Ball?

Vintage Base Ball is a pastime providing a glimpse into Civil War era recreation. Originally established in 1858, it is a gentleman's game with unique rules, terminology, uniforms, and a relaxed style of play.

What are the rules?

Some rules and customs are as follows:

  • Matches are conducted according to the highest standards of sportsmanship, gentlemanly behavior, courtesy and respect for others.
  • Swearing, spitting, scratching, consuming alcohol, chewing tobacco and wagering are prohibited behaviors that may result in a fine.
  • Players do not wear gloves or other protective equipment.
  • Lemon Peel balls, made of soft leather are used.
  • Only wooden bats are permitted.
  • The ball must be hurled underhanded and as near as possible over the center of home plate.
  • Swinging strikes are called and counted but balls are not.
  • A striker is out if a batted ball, fair or foul, is caught on the fly or on one bounce off the ground.
  • Bunting is not allowed.
  • A Runner may not advance if the strikerâ€™s ball is caught on the fly.
  • Infielders must start within one stride of their bag.
  • There is no sliding, leading off or stealing bases.

Who can play?

Vintage Base Ball Clubs typically welcome players of all backgrounds and skill levels. Although women in the 19th century often formed their own teams, the Plowboys welcome any interested parties, age 16 or older, to join the fun.
